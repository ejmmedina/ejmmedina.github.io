Network Analysis of the Primary Healthcare Facility Referral System in the Philippines

By: Elijah Justin Medina and Jomilynn Rebanal

Motivation

With the Universal Health Care Law, the healthcare system of the Philippines is expected to experience an upsurge in demand, possibly even to the point of overloading of the system. To create any preventive measures or policies, it is imperative to understand the capabilities of our healthcare system, first. In this study, we analyzed the referral system between different healthcare facilities in the Philippines.

Summary

Introduction: An effective health facility referral system enables timely and comprehensive access to healthcare services for the citizens. In this study, we performed a network analysis on the primary health facility referral system from barangay health stations (BHS) to rural health units (RHU) to hospitals.
Data:The dataset was comprised of 22,644 BHS, 2,592 RHUs, and 2,138 hospitals.
Methodology: We measured the distance between referring and referral facilities and imputed missing values with the average distance in the city/municipality or province. A tripartite network was created wherein edges were based on the existence of referral mechanism between facilities and weighted by distance. We compared regional hub importance and average shortest path lengths and we simulated the influx of referrals from RHUs to hospitals.
Results and Discussion: In general, regions in Mindanao have the highest average shortest path lengths. Critical hospital hubs were identified in Regions III, IV-A, and VIII but the first two regions demonstrate relatively low average shortest path lengths. On the other hand, the simulations show high critical population for NCR while the other regions show similar thresholds.
Conclusions: Regional variations exist in the primary healthcare facility referral mechanisms in the Philippines.
Recommendations: The Department of Health can use the results of this study in conjunction with the demand for healthcare to identify priority areas for its Health Facility Enhancement Program.
